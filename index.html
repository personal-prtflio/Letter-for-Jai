Dear Jai,

I’ve been thinking about this for a long time, and this is really hard for me to say, not because I don’t care, but because I do.

I need to be honest with you. I’ve really tried to make this work. I tried to show up, to adjust, to push myself to be better. But even after all that, I still can’t see or visualize a future where I can fully be what you need and deserve. And that truth scares me, because I know how deeply you feel.

Please understand this — this isn’t about you lacking anything. You didn’t fail me. You didn’t love me wrong. I love you, and that’s real. But love alone isn’t enough if I can’t bring myself up to the level of certainty, consistency, and emotional capacity that this relationship requires.

It feels like I was caught up in the situation — in the emotions, in wanting to be there, in not wanting to hurt you — and I mistook that for readiness. I wanted so badly to be sure, but the more I tried, the clearer it became that I’m not able to fully give myself the way a partner should.

Maybe we’re not really compatible in the ways that matter long-term. And as painful as that is to admit, it would be more unfair to keep holding on when I already know I can’t step into this future with confidence.

I don’t want to keep you in a place of uncertainty. You deserve someone who is sure — someone who doesn’t have to convince themselves to stay, someone who can see you in their future without doubt.

This decision hurts me too. Letting go doesn’t mean I never cared — it means I care enough to stop pretending I can be something I’m not ready to be.

So with honesty, and with love, I need to ask:

Will you not be my girlfriend?

I’m sorry for the pain this causes. I truly am.
